Windward argues that protecting subsea cables requires behavioral intelligence rather than AIS-only monitoring, as Q1 2026 data showed 2,700 extended-presence vessel events and 2,202 flag-of-convenience activities near cable routes. The company contends that the operational pattern before an incident is often more predictive than whether the resulting damage is later labeled accidental or deliberate, with the Indian Ocean accounting for 63% of service vessel cable-proximate activity. Their multi-source intelligence framework integrating SAR, EO, RF, and AIS aims to shift cable protection from reactive monitoring to predictive prevention.
